# Interface Overview

Orion's interface is designed to be intuitive and efficient. The layout is divided into several key areas that work together to provide a seamless creative experience.

## The Dashboard

When you first launch Orion, you are greeted by the **Dashboard**. This is your central hub for project management.

- **Recents**: Access your most recent projects with a single click.
- **Templates**: Start a new project using one of our curated templates (Studio, Scoring, Electronic).
- **Quick Start**: A dynamic rail that guides you through the first steps of a new project.

---

## The Timeline (ARRANGER)

The **Timeline** is where your arrangement takes shape. It is a high-resolution canvas for audio and MIDI clips.

- **Ruler**: Displays bars, beats, or time. Click and drag to set loop regions.
- **Tracks**: Manage your audio and instrument tracks. Each track features dedicated Mute, Solo, and Record-Arm controls.
- **Toolbox**: Quickly switch between the **Select**, **Blade**, **Glue**, and **Erase** tools.

---

## The Mixer

The **Mixer** is the heart of your signal flow. It provides a comprehensive view of all audio levels and processing.

- **Channel Strips**: Each track has a dedicated channel strip with a high-precision level meter, panning, and fader.
- **Inserts**: Add up to 8 real-time effects to any track.
- **Sends**: Route audio to auxiliary buses for parallel processing like reverb and delay.

---

## The Inspector

The **Inspector** is a context-aware panel that provides detailed control over the currently selected object.

- **Clip Inspector**: Adjust clip gain, fading, transposition, and stretching.
- **Track Inspector**: Access advanced routing, colors, and automation settings.
- **Plugin Inspector**: View and edit the parameters of your instruments and effects.

---

> [!NOTE]
> All panels are dockable and resizable. You can detach them into separate windows if you are working with multiple monitors.
