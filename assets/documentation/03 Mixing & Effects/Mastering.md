# Mastering Basics

Mastering is the final step in the production process where you polish the stereo mix and prepare it for distribution.

## The Goal of Mastering

Mastering isn't about "fixing the mix"—that should be done in the mixing stage. It's about:
- **Consistency**: Ensuring the track sounds good on all playback systems.
- **Loudness**: Bringing the track up to commercial volume standards.
- **Flow**: Making sure multiple tracks in an album feel cohesive.

---

## A Typical Mastering Chain

1. **Clean-up EQ**: Use a high-pass filter (around 30Hz) to remove sub-harmonic rumble.
2. **Subtle Compression**: A "Glue" compressor with a low ratio (1.5:1) to tighten the mix.
3. **Tonal Balance**: A broad-stroke EQ to add a bit of "air" (high shelf) or "warmth" (low-mid).
4. **Saturation**: Add harmonic excitement for a more "expensive" sound.
5. **Limiter**: The final stage. Prevent clipping while maximizing loudness.

---

## Metering & Loudness Standards

Orion features built-in LUFS and True Peak metering on the Master Channel.

- **Streaming (Spotify/Apple)**: Target around -14 LUFS Integrated.
- **True Peak**: Ensure your peaks never exceed -1.0 dBTP to avoid distortion during MP3/AAC conversion.

---

## Final Export

When your master is ready:
1. Press `Cmd/Ctrl + E`.
2. Format: **WAV**.
3. Sample Rate: **44.1 kHz**.
4. Bit Depth: **24-bit** (enable Dithering if the source was 32-bit).

> [!TIP]
> Always listen to your master at a low volume. If it sounds exciting and balanced when quiet, it will sound massive when cranked.
