# Automation

Automation allows you to change parameters over time, adding life and movement to your tracks.

## Creating Automation

Almost every parameter in Orion—from volume and panning to plugin settings—can be automated.

1. **Show Automation**: Click the "A" icon on a track header to expand the automation lanes.
2. **Select Parameter**: Use the dropdown menu on the lane to choose what to automate.
3. **Draw**: Click to add points, then drag them to create curves.

---

## Automation Modes

- **Read**: The track follows existing automation.
- **Write**: Overwrites any existing automation as you move the parameter in real-time.
- **Touch**: Only writes automation while you are actively holding/moving a control.
- **Latch**: Starts writing when you move a control and continues until you stop playback.

---

## Editing Curves

- **Smooth Curves**: Hover over a segment between two points and drag the "Bezier" handle to create a curve.
- **Selection**: Drag a marquee over multiple points to move them together.
- **Precision**: Click a point and enter a specific value in the Inspector.

---

## Automation Clips

In Orion, automation can also be contained within **Automation Clips**. These act like regular clips and can be moved, copied, and looped across the timeline independently of the audio/MIDI data.

> [!TIP]
> You can "Lock" automation to clips so that when you move an audio clip, its automation moves with it.
