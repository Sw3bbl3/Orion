# Tempo & The Grid

Music in Orion is organized around a time-based grid. Understanding how to manipulate tempo and signatures is essential for tight arrangements.

## Setting Project Tempo

- **Transport Bar**: Click the BPM value in the center of the transport to type a new tempo.
- **Tap Tempo**: Click the "TAP" button in time with your track to set the BPM naturally.
- **Tempo Map**: You can automate the project tempo via the **Global Tempo Track** at the top of the timeline.

---

## Time Signatures

- Change the time signature (e.g., 4/4, 3/4, 7/8) in the transport bar.
- Like tempo, time signatures can change throughout a project using **Signatures Markers**.

---

## The Smart Grid

Orion's grid is "Smart"—it adjusts its resolution based on your zoom level.

- **Adaptive Zoom**: When zoomed out, the grid shows Bars. As you zoom in, it reveals Beats (1/4), 1/8ths, 1/16ths, and even 1/128ths.
- **Triplet/Swing**: Toggle the grid mode to "Triplet" or "Dotted" for swing-based genres.
- **Swing Amount**: Adjust the global swing percentage in the project settings to add a human feel to MIDI notes.

---

## Quantization

Quantization aligns your MIDI or Audio transients to the nearest grid line.

1. Select the clips or notes you want to fix.
2. Press `Q`.
3. Adjust the **Strength** in the Inspector if you want to keep some of the original "performance feel."

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Audio Quantization (Warping) requires "High Quality" mode to be enabled in the clip inspector to prevent artifacts.
