# Arrangement & Editing

The Arranger is where you build the structure of your song. Orion provides powerful tools for manipulating clips and sections.

## Working with Clips

Clips are the building blocks of your project. They can contain audio data or MIDI notes.

- **Move**: Click and drag a clip to move it in time or to another track.
- **Resize**: Drag the left or right edge of a clip to trim it.
- **Loop**: Drag the upper right corner of a clip to loop it indefinitely.

---

## The Toolbox

Access these tools at the top of the Timeline or via keyboard shortcuts:

| Tool | Shortcut | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Select** | `1` | The default tool for moving and selecting. |
| **Blade** | `2` | Cut a clip at any point. |
| **Glue** | `3` | Merge multiple clips into one. |
| **Erase** | `4` | Click to delete clips or sections. |

---

## Snapping & Grid

Orion features a "Smart Grid" that adapts as you zoom in and out. 

- **Snap to Grid**: When enabled, clips will snap to the nearest bar, beat, or subdivision.
- **Grid Resolution**: Can be set to absolute values (e.g., 1/16) or "Adaptive".
- **Nudge**: Move selected clips by very small increments using `Shift + Arrows`.

---

## Markers & Sections

Use **Markers** to label different sections of your song (Intro, Verse, Chorus).

1. Click the **Marker Track** at the top of the timeline.
2. Press `M` to add a marker at the current playhead position.
3. Name your markers to stay organized.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Markers can also be used as "Snap-to" points, making it easy to align clips with song sections.
