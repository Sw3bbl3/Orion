# Audio Glossary

A quick guide to the technical terminology used throughout Orion and the audio industry.

### ADSR
**Attack, Decay, Sustain, Release**. The four stages of a sound's volume or filter envelope.

### Bit Depth
The resolution of the audio signal. Higher bit depths (24-bit, 32-bit) provide a lower noise floor and better dynamic range.

### Clipping
Distortion that occurs when an audio signal exceeds the maximum level a system can handle (0dB).

### DAW
**Digital Audio Workstation**. The software environment (like Orion) used for recording, editing, and producing audio.

### Dithering
Noise added to a signal when reducing bit depth (e.g., 32-bit to 16-bit) to prevent quantization errors.

### Latency
The delay between when an audio signal enters a system and when it exits. Measured in milliseconds (ms).

### LUFS
**Loudness Units relative to Full Scale**. The industry standard for measuring perceived loudness.

### MIDI
**Musical Instrument Digital Interface**. A protocol that allows instruments and computers to communicate by sending note and control data (not audio).

### Panning
The distribution of an audio signal in a stereo or multi-channel sound field.

### Sample Rate
The number of times per second that audio is sampled. Standard rates include 44.1kHz (CD quality) and 48kHz (Video standard).

### VST / AU
**Virtual Studio Technology / Audio Units**. Industry standards for software plugins like instruments and effects.
